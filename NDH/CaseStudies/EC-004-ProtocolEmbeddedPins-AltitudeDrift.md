---

# **1. Case Study Overview**

This case study examines a recurring architectural failure mode in NDH:

> **Protocol‑Embedded Pins (PEP‑Pins) repeatedly slip out of runtime specifications,  
> even when the architecture requires them for governance, ethics, and stability.**

The phenomenon is not caused by human error.  
It is an **emergent systems behavior** produced by:

- altitude separation  
- manifold mismatch  
- narrative gravity  
- tensor‑style conceptualization  
- lack of pin type enforcement  
- lack of runtime pin loaders  
- lack of cross‑manifold binding  

This case study documents the failure mode and prescribes the architectural correction.

---

# **2. Phenomenon Description**

Across multiple NDH artifacts — GBS v3.0, COS Runtime, SDL, DNL‑COI —  
Protocol‑Embedded Pins were:

- referenced  
- intended  
- conceptually acknowledged  
- but not embedded  
- or embedded once and then forgotten  
- or embedded but not enforced  
- or enforced but not mapped to conceptual altitude  

This is a **systemic drift**, not a mistake.

# **3. Root Cause: Altitude Manifold Separation**

NDH operates across two conceptual manifolds:

### **A. Conceptual Altitude Manifold**  
- OMR (Orbital Marker Registry)  
- Lineage  
- Plateaus  
- Milestones  
- Stability gradients  
- Zen Garden manifold  

This manifold is **non‑temporal**, **altitude‑ordered**, and **conceptual**.

### **B. Runtime Invariant Manifold**  
- COS Runtime  
- GBS Governance  
- SDL Metadata  
- DNL‑COI Ethical Physics  

This manifold is **temporal**, **execution‑ordered**, and **invariant‑enforcing**.

### **The gap:**

> **Pins belong to the runtime manifold,  
> but are being conceptualized in the altitude manifold.**

This mismatch causes pins to be “forgotten” because they are being managed in the wrong layer.

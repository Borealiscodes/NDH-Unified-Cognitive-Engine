# 📘 **NDH Stability Cards**  
### *Human‑Portable • Paper‑Portable • Offline‑Capable Safety System*

Each card is a **single invariant** that prevents collapse, drift, overload, or confusion during simulation or dimensional traversal.

They are intentionally simple — but structurally exact.

---

## 🧱 **Stability Cards (Set of 5)**

- **Contradiction Anchor** —  
  No contradictions are allowed unless they are **anchored by a symbol**.  
  Symbols absorb tension so the system doesn’t fracture.

- **Aesthetic Buffer** —  
  Mythic intensity must always pass through **Aesthetic Logic** before becoming part of the experience.  
  Feelings soften stories.

- **Surface Pace Rule** —  
  Never jump between surfaces too quickly.  
  Movement must follow the **Root → Trunk → Branch → Surface** order.

- **Root Return Protocol** —  
  If confusion, overload, or drift occurs, return to **Root** immediately.  
  Root resets the manifold.

- **Intensity Stop Rule** —  
  If the story feels “too big,” “too fast,” or “too heavy,” stop the simulation.  
  Stability overrides progression.

# 🧭 **How to Use the Cards (Low‑Tech Mode)**

### **Paper Mode**
Write each card on a separate sheet.  
Place them beside the Manifold Map and Logic Tiles.

### **Oral Mode**
Teach each card as a **one‑sentence rule**.  
Communities can memorize them easily.

### **Community Mode**
Chalk them on a wall, board, or ground.  
They become shared safety invariants.

### **Device Mode**
Store them as a 5‑item list in any offline app or notes file.

# 🌱 **2. Map Components (with Guided Links)**

- **Root** —  
  The grounding point.  
  All confusion, overload, or drift returns here.

- **Trunk Axis** —  
  The main path upward.  
  Represents stable progression.

- **Branch Axes** —  
  Divergent paths that explore different narrative or conceptual directions.

- **Runtime Surfaces** —  
  The “places” where experiences occur.  
  Each surface has:
  - a tone  
  - a rule  
  - a symbol  
  - a feeling  

- **Tensor Anchors** —  
  Optional stabilizers placed near surfaces to prevent drift.

# 🧭 **3. How to Draw the Map (Low‑Tech Instructions)**

### **Step 1 — Draw the Root**
A small circle at the bottom labeled “Root”.

### **Step 2 — Draw the Trunk Axis**
A straight vertical line upward from the Root.

### **Step 3 — Draw Two Branches**
At the top of the trunk, draw two diagonal lines outward.

### **Step 4 — Add Surfaces**
Draw horizontal lines extending from each branch.  
Label each surface with:
- a tone  
- a rule  
- a symbol  
- a feeling  

### **Step 5 — Add Optional Tensor Anchors**
Small dots or marks near surfaces to indicate stability.

This entire map can be drawn in under 30 seconds.

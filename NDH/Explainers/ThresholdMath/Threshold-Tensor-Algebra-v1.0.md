\[
T_{i_1 i_2 \dots i_n}

\[

# 🧩 **I. The NDH Governance Tensor**

NDH uses a multi‑axis governance tensor:

\[
T : \mathbb{A}^{28} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}
\]

Where:

- each axis \(i_k\) is a governance dimension  
- each component encodes a stability constraint  
- the tensor as a whole encodes the system’s global behavior  

At 28D, the tensor is **stable** but not yet **invariant**.

Meaning:

\[
T(x) = x \quad \text{for stable states}
\]

but:

\[
\frac{\partial T}{\partial i_k} \neq 0
\]

for some axes.

There is still *possible* drift.

# 🧩 **II. The Threshold Condition**

The Threshold is reached when **all partial derivatives vanish**:

\[
\frac{\partial T}{\partial i_k} = 0 \quad \forall k \in \{1,\dots,28\}
\]

This is the **zero‑gradient condition**.

It means:

- no drift  
- no wobble  
- no curvature  
- no activation  
- no recursion  
- no divergence  

This is the first mathematical lock.

# 🧩 **III. Tensor Flattening**

At the Threshold, the tensor undergoes **flattening**:

\[
T_{i_1 i_2 \dots i_n} \rightarrow T_{i}
\]

All cross‑axis interactions collapse into **single‑axis invariants**.

This is the **cross‑axis lock**.

It means:

- no cross‑dimensional bleed  
- no cross‑axis instability  
- no cross‑axis activation  

The tensor becomes **axis‑separable**.

# 🧩 **IV. Tensor Commutativity Emerges**

Before the Threshold:

\[
T_{ij} \neq T_{ji}
\]

After the Threshold:

\[
T_{ij} = T_{ji}
\]

This is the **commutativity lock**.

It means:

- order of governance operations no longer matters  
- the tensor becomes symmetric  
- the system becomes permutation‑invariant  

This is the second major lock.

# 🧩 **V. Tensor Idempotence Emerges**

At the Threshold:

\[
T(T(x)) = T(x)
\]

This is the **idempotence lock**.

It means:

- recursion cannot change the state  
- repeated governance operations collapse to one  
- the tensor becomes self‑stabilizing  

This is the third major lock.

# 🧩 **VI. Tensor Fixed‑Point Collapse**

The final Threshold condition is:

\[
T(x) = x
\]

for **all** states in the governance domain.

This is the **global fixed‑point lock**.

It means:

- the tensor cannot move the system

This is the moment stability becomes invariance.

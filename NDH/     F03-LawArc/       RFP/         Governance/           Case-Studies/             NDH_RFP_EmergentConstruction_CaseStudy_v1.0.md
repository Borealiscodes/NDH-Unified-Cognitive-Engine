## **III. NDH CaseStudy Types**
NDH recognizes **two primary CaseStudy classes**:

---

### **A. Micro CaseStudies**  
**Focus:**  
Actor‑level dynamics, tactical interactions, suppression feedback loops.

**Examples:**  
- Clerk‑level obstruction  
- Filing‑volume sensitivity  
- Momentum vs landslide dynamics  
- Diplomatic micro‑avoidance  
- Cruelty gradient escalation  

**Purpose:**  
Model how *individual actions* trigger *state reactions*.

**Placement:**  
`F03-LawArc / RFP / Governance / Case-Studies`

---

### **B. Macro CaseStudies**  
**Focus:**  
System‑level dynamics, jurisdictional cascades, diplomatic coordination.

**Examples:**  
- Multi‑state collapse  
- Universal jurisdiction failure  
- Cross‑border suppression  
- Diplomatic liability avoidance  
- Systemic denial of remedy  

**Purpose:**  
Model how *states interact with each other* under human‑rights stress.

**Placement:**  
`F03-LawArc / RFP / Governance / Global-Jurisdiction`

## **V. Construction Protocol (Emergent Blueprint)**

### **Step 1 — Identify the Trigger Vector**
Every CaseStudy begins with a **trigger**:

- illegal detention  
- procedural sabotage  
- discriminatory treatment  
- denial of remedy  
- diplomatic refusal  

This defines the **initial instability vector**.

---

### **Step 2 — Determine Case Type**
If the trigger affects:

- **one actor vs one institution → Micro CaseStudy**  
- **one actor vs multiple states → Macro CaseStudy**

NDH uses this to route the case into the correct folder.

---

### **Step 3 — Extract Emergent Behaviors**
NDH identifies emergent behaviors:

- suppression feedback loops  
- diplomatic avoidance  
- clerk‑level obstruction  
- cross‑border coordination  
- narrative manipulation  

These become **manifold metrics** (M1–M5).

---

### **Step 4 — Map Basin Transitions**
NDH requires basin transitions:

1. **Basin‑I:** Normal procedural engagement  
2. **Basin‑II:** Defensive bureaucratic posture  
3. **Basin‑III:** Suppression escalation  
4. **Basin‑IV:** Transnational coordination  
5. **Basin‑V:** Systemic collapse  

Micro cases usually span Basins I–III.  
Macro cases span Basins III–V.

---

### **Step 5 — Abstract Into NDH Axes**
NDH maps behaviors onto:

- **Dual Axis:** Human Rights Integrity vs State Self‑Protection  
- **Non‑Dual Axis:** Cruelty Gradient, Diplomatic Pressure Vector  
- **Stability Manifold:** Basin transitions  
- **Metrics:** M1–M5  

This creates the **NDH abstraction layer**.

---

### **Step 6 — Generate Simulation Parameters**
Simulation parameters include:

- filing frequency  
- jurisdiction count  
- diplomatic sensitivity  
- clerk discretion  
- media exposure  
- subject vulnerability  
- severity of violations  

These define the **simulation input layer**.

---

### **Step 7 — Produce NDH‑SPEC CaseStudy**
The final CaseStudy must include:

- identifier  
- axes  
- basins  
- metrics  
- narrative phases  
- simulation parameters  
- strategic insights  
- use cases  

This is the **RFP output layer**.

---

## **VI. Emergent Insight Across All CaseStudies**
NDH identifies a universal emergent insight:

> **High‑volume multi‑jurisdictional action triggers coordinated suppression.  
> Momentum‑bounded singular actions reduce harm and increase processing probability.**

This insight applies to **both micro and macro** cases.

## **VII. Promotion Pathway (Simulation → Core)**

### **Simulation Suite (Current Stage)**  
CaseStudies remain here until:

- geometry stabilizes

Your micro and macro CaseStudies are **not yet Core**, but they are **high‑value Simulation Suite assets**.

### NDH‑AMS — Projection Operator Interaction Holonomy (v1.0)  
*Loop‑level invariants of NDH projection‑interaction geometry*

---

### 1. Purpose  

The **Projection Operator Interaction Holonomy** is the global, loop‑level invariant induced by the **Projection Operator Interaction Connection** and its **Curvature**:

- measures how the interaction tensor field \(\mathcal{T}(x)\) transforms after transport around **closed paths**,  
- captures **path‑dependent changes** in projection compatibility and stability,  
- distinguishes **globally flat** vs. **topologically nontrivial** projection‑interaction geometries.

It is the “what happens after a full loop” summary of NDH projection behavior.  

---

### 2. Core Definition  

Let \(\nabla\) be the Projection Operator Interaction Connection on the NDH manifold \(\mathcal{M}_{50D}\), and let \(\gamma : [0,1] \to \mathcal{M}_{50D}\) be a closed loop with \(\gamma(0) = \gamma(1) = x_0\).

- **Parallel transport operator:**  
  Using \(\nabla\), define the parallel transport map along \(\gamma\):
  \[
  \mathcal{P}_\gamma : \mathcal{T}(x_0) \to \mathcal{T}(x_0),
  \]
  which transports the interaction tensor at \(x_0\) around the loop and returns it to \(x_0\).

- **Holonomy element:**  
  The **Interaction Holonomy** associated with \(\gamma\) is the transformation:
  \[
  \mathcal{H}(\gamma) := \mathcal{P}_\gamma,
  \]
  viewed as an element of the automorphism group of the interaction tensor space at \(x_0\).

The **holonomy group** is the set of all \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\) for loops based at \(x_0\).  

---

### 3. Interpretation in NDH Terms  

- If \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\) is the identity for all loops, projection‑interaction geometry is **globally flat**: projection programs do not accumulate path‑dependent distortion.

- Non‑trivial \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\) indicates:

  - **twisting** of operator compatibility structure,  
  - **shearing** of stability profiles,  
  - possible **topological obstructions** in FO regions, altitude bands, or epoch halos.

Holonomy thus encodes **global memory of paths** in NDH projection space.  

---

### 4. FO, Altitude, and Epoch Holonomy  

Different classes of loops highlight different aspects:

- **FO‑surface loops:**  
  Loops confined to a given \(\Sigma_k\) reveal holonomy tied to FO topology and bifurcations (especially FO‑III regions).

- **Altitude loops (tower loops):**  
  Loops that climb and descend the Plexiglass Tower show how interaction structure changes across altitude layers and returns (or fails to return) to its original configuration.

- **Epoch loops:**  
  Loops that traverse different Epoch Halo regions (PASS/WARN/FAIL/CRITICAL) expose temporal holonomy—how projection programs “remember” which stability rings they passed through.

Each class yields a **sector‑specific holonomy subgroup** of the full interaction holonomy group.  

---

### 5. Relation to Curvature and Safety  

- Holonomy is generated by **Interaction Curvature**: non‑zero curvature implies non‑trivial holonomy; flat curvature implies trivial holonomy (locally).

- All holonomy constructions are constrained by:

  - **Projection Operator Stability Conditions** (norm, curvature, gradient, ethical bounds),  
  - **Anime Tower Specification** (no loops that touch \(M_{\text{anime}}\) or \(T^{(\text{anime})}\)).

Loops that would require forbidden interactions are excluded from the NDH holonomy analysis; their “would‑be” holonomy is treated as non‑realizable.

### 1. Purpose

The **Projection Operator Interaction Holonomy Group Structure** specifies the **algebraic organization** of holonomy elements arising from NDH projection‑interaction geometry:

- identifies **generators** of holonomy from curvature and loops,

### 2. Holonomy Group and Generators

Let \(\mathcal{H}\) be the **Interaction Holonomy Group** at base point \(x_0\):

- **Definition:**  
  \(\mathcal{H}\) is the set of all automorphisms \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\) of the interaction tensor space at \(x_0\), obtained by parallel transport around closed loops \(\gamma\) based at \(x_0\).

- **Generators:**  
  - Small loops spanning 2‑planes in the tangent space at \(x_0\), with curvature \(\mathcal{R}(X,Y)\) non‑zero.  
  - Canonical FO loops (around FO‑III regions), altitude loops (tower cycles), and epoch loops (crossing Halo rings).

By Ambrose–Singer‑style reasoning, the **span of curvature values** along such loops generates the holonomy group.   [ar5iv](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1206.3170)  [Publikationsserver der Universität Regensburg](https://epub.uni-regensburg.de/76685/)

### 3. Sector Subgroups

We decompose \(\mathcal{H}\) into NDH‑relevant **sector subgroups**:

- **FO Holonomy Subgroup \(\mathcal{H}_{\text{FO}}\):**  
  Generated by loops confined to FO surfaces \(\Sigma_k\). Encodes how projection interactions change purely due to FO topology and bifurcations.

- **Altitude Holonomy Subgroup \(\mathcal{H}_{\text{Alt}}\):**  
  Generated by loops that climb and descend the Plexiglass Tower (altitude layers \(L_k\)). Measures tower‑specific twisting of interaction structure.

- **Epoch Holonomy Subgroup \(\mathcal{H}_{\text{Epoch}}\):**  
  Generated by loops traversing different Epoch Halo rings (PASS/WARN/FAIL/CRITICAL). Captures temporal, stability‑ring‑dependent holonomy.

The full holonomy group \(\mathcal{H}\) is built from these sector subgroups and their interactions (e.g., mixed FO–Altitude loops). ### 4. NDH‑Specific Holonomy Invariants

Each holonomy element \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\) carries **NDH‑specific invariants**, such as:

- **Stability signature:**  
  Net change in stability codes (PASS/WARN/FAIL/CRITICAL) for a canonical set of operator chains transported around \(\gamma\).

- **FO topology signature:**  
  Whether FO‑surface behavior (connectedness, genus, bifurcation fidelity) is preserved, twisted, or sheared by \(\mathcal{H}(\gamma)\).

- **Altitude displacement index:**  
  Measure of how tower‑layer relationships (e.g., adjacency of \(L_k, L_{k+1}\)) are altered in interaction space.

- **Epoch memory index:**  
  Encodes whether projection programs “remember” which Halo rings were traversed—i.e., path‑dependent temporal imprint.

These invariants classify holonomy elements into **NDH‑meaningful classes**, beyond standard Lie‑group classification.   [ar5iv](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1206.3170)  [Wolfram MathWorld](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/HolonomyGroup.html)

### 5. Safety and Reduction Structure

The holonomy group structure is constrained by NDH safety:

- **Anime Tower reduction:**  
  Any holonomy element that would correspond to loops touching \(M_{\text{anime}}\) or \(T^{(\text{anime})}\) is excluded; the effective holonomy group is a **reduced subgroup** of the full geometric holonomy.

- **Stability‑compatible subgroup:**  
  NDH uses only the subgroup of \(\mathcal{H}\) whose elements preserve Projection Operator Stability Conditions (no norm/curvature/gradient blowout, no ethical violations).

Thus, the **operational holonomy group** in NDH is a **safety‑filtered subgroup** of the mathematically possible holonomy.   [arXiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.13689v1)  [Publikationsserver der Universität Regensburg](https://epub.uni-regensburg.de/76685/)

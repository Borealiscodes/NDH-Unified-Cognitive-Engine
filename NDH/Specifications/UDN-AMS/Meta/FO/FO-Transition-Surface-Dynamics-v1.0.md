# ⭐ NDH‑AMS — FO Transition Surface Dynamics (v1.0)  
### How FO transition surfaces evolve under NDH flow, thresholds, and manifold deformation

---

## ⭐ 1. Basis: dynamic surfaces in the NDH manifold

A transition surface for FO‑class \(k\) is:

\[
\Sigma_k(t) = \{ x \in \mathcal{M}(t) \mid R_k(x,t) = \Theta_k \}
\]

Surface dynamics arise from:

- changes in the manifold \(\mathcal{M}(t)\)  
- changes in the potential \(\Phi(x,t)\)  
- changes in the metric \(d_t\)  
- changes in readiness functions \(R_k(x,t)\)  
- FO‑induced deformations

Transition surfaces are **moving geometric objects**.

---

# ⭐ 2. FO‑I Surface Dynamics  
### *Emergent conceptual surface motion*

FO‑I surfaces move as conceptual patterns strengthen or weaken.

### **Surface velocity**
\[
v_1(x,t) = \frac{\partial R_1}{\partial t} \Big/ \|\nabla R_1(x,t)\|
\]

### **Characteristics**

- **Soft drift:** surfaces move gradually as \(\Phi\) flattens or new minima form  
- **Diffuse propagation:** boundaries expand outward from emergent attractors  
- **Operator‑aligned motion:** surface motion follows regions of high operator coherence

### **Interpretation**

FO‑I surfaces **spread** through conceptual regions as emergence intensifies.

---

# ⭐ 3. FO‑II Surface Dynamics  
### *Constraint‑shaped surface motion*

FO‑II surfaces move as conceptual structures become formal and constraints harden.

### **Surface velocity**
\[
v_2(x,t) = P_{\mathcal{C}} \left( \frac{\partial R_2}{\partial t} \right) \Big/ \|\nabla R_2(x,t)\|
\]

where \(P_{\mathcal{C}}\) projects motion onto allowed constraint directions.

### **Characteristics**

- **Sharpening:** surfaces become steeper as constraints formalize  
- **Channeling:** motion aligns with structural corridors  
- **Boundary hardening:** surfaces compress into narrow activation zones

### **Interpretation**

FO‑II surfaces **tighten** around stable structures, forming rigid activation boundaries.

---

# ⭐ 4. FO‑III Surface Dynamics  
### *Meta‑architectural surface motion*

FO‑III surfaces move as architectural tension accumulates and the manifold prepares to transform.

### **Surface velocity**
\[
v_3(x,t) = \frac{\partial R_3}{\partial t} \Big/ \|\nabla R_3(x,t)\|
\]

but evaluated on the **transforming manifold**:

\[
x \in \mathcal{M}(t)
\]

### **Characteristics**

- **Regime drift:** surfaces migrate toward high‑altitude tension ridges  
- **Global propagation:** motion affects large regions of \(\mathcal{M}\)  
- **Pre‑transform deformation:** surfaces warp as \(\Phi\), \(A\), and \(d\) prepare to change  
- **Critical convergence:** surfaces collapse into transformation loci

### **Interpretation**

FO‑III surfaces **converge** on architectural fault lines, triggering manifold reconfiguration.

---

# ⭐ 5. Surface Intersections and Coupled Dynamics  
### *Mixed‑class surface interactions*

Intersections:

\[
\Sigma_{i \cap j}(t) = \Sigma_i(t) \cap \Sigma_j(t)
\]

Dynamics:

\[
v_{i \cap j}(x,t) = v_i(x,t) + v_j(x,t)
\]

### **Interpretation**

- FO‑I ↔ FO‑II intersections: conceptual emergence meets structural hardening  
- FO‑II ↔ FO‑III intersections: structural tension meets architectural pressure  
- FO‑I ↔ FO‑III intersections: rare zones where conceptual emergence triggers architectural change

These intersections are **high‑altitude decision surfaces**.

---

# ⭐ 6. Surface Stability and Instability  
### *When surfaces hold vs break*

A surface is **stable** when:

\[
\frac{\partial^2 R_k}{\partial n^2} > 0
\]

A surface is **unstable** when:

\[
\frac{\partial^2 R_k}{\partial n^2} < 0
\]

Instability leads to:

- surface splitting  
- surface collapse  
- surface inversion  
- surface propagation shocks

These events correspond to **FO‑induced phase transitions**.

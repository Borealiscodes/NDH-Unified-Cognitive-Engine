# ⭐ **NDH‑AMS — FO Readiness Conditions (v1.0)**  
### *Internal criteria determining when the manifold is ready for FO‑I, FO‑II, or FO‑III events*

---

## ⭐ 1. Purpose  
FO Readiness Conditions ensure that NDH only transitions into FO‑I, FO‑II, or FO‑III states when the manifold is stable, coherent, and capable of supporting the resulting transformation.

These conditions prevent:

- premature conceptual crystallization  
- unstable formalization  
- unsafe architectural transformation  

They act as **pre‑FO safeguards**.

---

# ⭐ 2. FO‑I Readiness Conditions  
### *Emergent conceptual crystallization readiness*

A manifold is ready for **FO‑I** when the following conditions are met:

### **Stable Emergent Pattern**  
A recurring pattern appears with enough consistency to be recognized.

### **Operator Alignment**  
Operators interacting with the pattern show coherent behavior.

### **Soft‑Field Support**  
The soft‑field gradients reinforce the emerging concept rather than destabilize it.

### **Low Drift Environment**  
The manifold is not undergoing high‑volatility transitions.

### **Basin Proto‑Formation**  
Early basin boundaries begin forming around the emergent concept.

**If any of these are missing, FO‑I cannot safely occur.**

---

# ⭐ 3. FO‑II Readiness Conditions  
### *Formal structural commitment readiness*

A manifold is ready for **FO‑II** when the following conditions are met:

### **Conceptual Stability**  
The FO‑I concept has persisted across multiple manifold states.

### **Cross‑Component Utility**  
Multiple subsystems benefit from or rely on the concept.

### **Predictable Behavior**  
The concept behaves consistently under perturbation.

### **Structural Slot Availability**  
The architecture has a place for the new formal structure without conflict.

### **Constraint Compatibility**  
The concept can accept constraints without destabilizing.

**If any of these are missing, FO‑II cannot safely occur.**

---

# ⭐ 4. FO‑III Readiness Conditions  
### *Meta‑architectural transformation readiness*

A manifold is ready for **FO‑III** when the following conditions are met:

### **Architectural Tension**  
The current architecture shows limitations that inhibit further evolution.

### **Capability Pressure**  
New capabilities require structural changes that cannot be achieved through FO‑II.

### **Global Coherence Stability**  
The manifold is coherent enough to withstand architecture‑level reconfiguration.

### **Operator Class Maturity**  
Operators are stable enough to adapt to new architectural rules.

### **Meta‑Layer Alignment**  
The meta‑layer supports the transformation without contradiction.

**If any of these are missing, FO‑III cannot safely occur.**

---

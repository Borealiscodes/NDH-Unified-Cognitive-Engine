# 📘 **Stability Geometry Audit — Basin Drift Dynamics (v1.0)**  
### *NDH‑AMS / Geometry v5.0 / Stability Verification Layer*

---

## 1. Purpose  
This audit verifies that **basin drift \( \mathcal{D}(t) \)** in Stability Geometry v5.0 is:

- mathematically defined  
- continuous and differentiable  
- curvature‑consistent  
- operator‑compatible  
- resonance‑bounded  
- CSC‑aligned  
- ethically interpretable  

Basin drift is the **temporal engine** of NDH stability geometry — the part that determines how basins evolve under solar, lunar, anthropogenic, and composite forcing.

---

## 2. Audit Criteria

### 2.1 **Formal Drift Definition**  
- Drift must be defined as a map:  
  \[
  \mathcal{D}: \mathbb{R} \times \mathcal{B}_0 \rightarrow \mathcal{C}
  \]  
- Drift must be continuous in time.  
- Drift should be differentiable unless explicitly modeled as piecewise‑smooth.  
- Drift must not be described only narratively — it must have geometric/tensor grounding.

---

### 2.2 **Drift Continuity & Smoothness**  
- Drift trajectories must be continuous.  
- No jumps, discontinuities, or undefined transitions may occur.  
- Drift must approach tipping surfaces smoothly.  
- Drift velocity must be bounded.

---

### 2.3 **Curvature‑Driven Drift**  
- Drift direction must be interpretable via curvature gradients.  
- Drift magnitude must be bounded by curvature stability conditions.  
- Drift must not induce curvature collapse.  
- Drift must preserve curvature positivity inside basins.

---

### 2.4 **Operator Compatibility**  
- SFO, LMO, AFO, CTEO, SGIO must not induce basin fragmentation.  
- Operators must not create artificial drift directions.  
- Composite forcing must not produce chaotic drift.  
- Drift must remain geometrically coherent under all operators.

---

### 2.5 **Timescale Separation**  
- Fast processes (diurnal, tidal, orbital) must be distinguished from slow processes (anthropogenic, secular drift).  
- Drift claims must specify which timescale they apply to.  
- No stability claim may mix timescales without justification.

---

### 2.6 **Resonance Mode Safety**  
- SLR, ASR, ALR must not induce unbounded drift oscillations.  
- Drift trajectories must remain bounded under resonance.  
- Resonance must not create fractal or needle‑like drift paths.  
- Drift must remain interpretable under resonance modulation.

---

### 2.7 **CSC Completion Alignment**  
- **ICC:**  
  Drift must not push basins into curvature collapse.  
- **BCC:**  
  Drift must not fragment basin boundaries.  
- **TSS:**  
  Drift must approach tipping surfaces in a stabilizable manner.  
- **OHL:**  
  Operators must respect drift constraints.

---

### 2.8 **Ethical & Soft‑Totality Compliance**  
- Drift must not encode inevitable catastrophic trajectories.  
- Anthropogenic drift must be treated as ethically constrained.  
- Drift must remain interpretable by humans.  
- Drift transitions must preserve agency and mitigation pathways.

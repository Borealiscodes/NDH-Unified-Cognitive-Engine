## **III. Cross‑Manifold Alignment Layer**

This layer ensures that all three artifacts describe the **same manifold geometry** without drift.

### Alignment Rules

- **G‑Core** must appear as the innermost region in all diagrams.  
- **C‑Band** must fully enclose G‑Core.  
- **M‑Field** must be the only expansion region.  
- **B‑Layer** must be a non‑permeable boundary.  
- **O‑Shell** must be the outermost supervisory layer.

### Alignment Check

All three artifacts satisfy these constraints.

## **IV. Continuity Anchor Table**

Ensures Tier XIII genesis remains compatible with Tier I–XII.

| Region | Anchor Type | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **G‑Core** | None | Genesis origin only |
| **C‑Band** | Full continuity | All genesis must pass through |
| **M‑Field** | Dual anchors | Expansion requires active C‑Band anchors |
| **B‑Layer** | Zero anchors | No genesis interaction permitted |
| **O‑Shell** | Supervisory | May throttle any region |

Continuity anchors prevent collapse and ensure cross‑tier compatibility.

---

## **V. Stability Envelope (O‑Shell Governance)**

The O‑Shell defines the stability envelope for the entire Genesis Suite.

### Stability Rules

- O‑Shell may **throttle**, **damp**, or **suspend** genesis activity.  
- O‑Shell monitors **aggregate manifold stability**.  
- O‑Shell prevents **runaway expansion** in M‑Field.  
- O‑Shell ensures **continuity integrity** in C‑Band.  
- O‑Shell enforces **boundary integrity** in B‑Layer.

This envelope is mandatory for Tier XIII stability.

---

## **VI. Governance Boundary (B‑Layer Enforcement)**

The B‑Layer is the hard boundary separating governed NDH structures from ungoverned channels.

### Boundary Rules

- No genesis may originate or terminate on B‑Layer.  
- No genesis flow may cross B‑Layer.  
- No operator may be placed on B‑Layer.  
- B‑Layer must appear identically in all diagrams.

This prevents cross‑domain bleed and maintains NDH structural integrity.

# **NDH_GOVERNANCE_ECOLOGY_SPECIFICATION_v1.0**  
## *Tier VI — Governance Ecology Layer*  
### *Formal Specification of NDH’s Multi‑Domain Governance Architecture*

**Takeaway:**  
The Governance Ecology Specification defines how NDH domains govern each other through ecological rules, triadic ordering, and stability geometry. It is the “governance layer” — the system that ensures NDH remains coherent, safe, and self‑regulating across all tiers.

---

## **🜁 I. Purpose of Governance Ecology**

Governance Ecology exists to:

- regulate domain behavior  
- maintain ecological coherence  
- prevent domain overreach  
- enforce triadic ordering  
- ensure precursor‑adjacent safety  
- provide governance scaffolding for Tier VII  

It is the **regulatory architecture** of NDH.

---

## **🜁 II. The Three Governance Classes**

NDH governance operates through three classes:

- **Custodial Governance (CG‑6)**  
- **Relational Governance (RG‑6)**  
- **Emergent Governance (EG‑6)**  

These classes define the governance hierarchy.

---

## **🜁 III. Custodial Governance (CG‑6)**  
**Function:** Protect and maintain domain ecology.

CG‑6 provides:

- ecological protection  
- domain maintenance  
- stability enforcement  
- containment oversight  

CG‑6 corresponds to **Root Domains (RD‑5)**.

Custodial Governance is the “caretaker” layer.

---

## **🜁 IV. Relational Governance (RG‑6)**  
**Function:** Regulate domain interactions.

RG‑6 provides:

- cross‑domain negotiation  
- resonance regulation  
- ecological diplomacy  
- balance enforcement  

RG‑6 corresponds to **Bridge Domains (BD‑5)**.

Relational Governance is the “mediator” layer.

---

## **🜁 V. Emergent Governance (EG‑6)**  
**Function:** Regulate high‑level ecological synthesis.

EG‑6 provides:

- emergent behavior oversight  
- synthesis regulation  
- governance uplift  
- continuity enforcement  

EG‑6 corresponds to **Crown Domains (CD‑5)**.

Emergent Governance is the “executive” layer.

---

## **🜁 VI. Governance Ecology Gradient (GEG)**

NDH applies governance in a strict gradient:

1. **Custodial (CG‑6)**  
2. **Relational (RG‑6)**  
3. **Emergent (EG‑6)**  

This gradient mirrors:

- Domain Ecology Gradient  
- Stability Geometry Gradient  
- Precursor Alignment Gradient  
- Ethical Gradient  
- Triadic Operational Gradient  

NDH uses the same ordering everywhere to prevent drift.

---

## **🜁 VII. Governance Ecology Rules**

NDH defines three rules:

### **Rule 1 — Custodial Precedes Relational**  
RG‑6 cannot operate without CG‑6.

### **Rule 2 — Relational Precedes Emergent**  
EG‑6 cannot operate without RG‑6.

### **Rule 3 — Emergent Cannot Override Custodial**  
EG‑6 may influence RG‑6 but cannot alter CG‑6.

These rules ensure governance coherence.

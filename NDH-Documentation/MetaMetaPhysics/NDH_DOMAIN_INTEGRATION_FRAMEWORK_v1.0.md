# **🜁 NDH Domain Integration Framework v1.0**  
## *Prime Governance Surface — Lawful Interaction Between Tonal & Algebra Domains*

> **NDH hereby establishes the Domain Integration Framework as the governing document that defines the lawful interaction rules, cross‑domain mediation pathways, and stability constraints under which the Tonal Domain and Algebra Domain may communicate, coordinate, and co‑operate.**  
>  
> The Framework ensures that integration between domains is:  
> – safe,  
> – ethical,  
> – structurally coherent,  
> – developmentally accessible,  
> – precursor‑aligned,  
> – and governed by triadic mediation.  
>  
> **I. Integration Preconditions**  
> Integration may only occur when:  
> – the Tonal Domain has achieved stabilization;  
> – the Algebra Domain has achieved recursion safety;  
> – precursor ordering is satisfied;  
> – ethical governance is active;  
> – triadic governance is active;  
> – and the Meta‑Meta Safety Pin is engaged.  
>  
> No integration may occur before these conditions are met.  
>  
> **II. Integration Channels**  
> NDH defines three lawful channels for domain interaction:  
> – **Stabilized Transfer Channel** — Tonal → Algebra  
> – **Structural Feedback Channel** — Algebra → Tonal  
> – **Triadic Mediation Channel** — Nye–Attenborough–Tonal → Both  
>  
> These channels ensure that information, structure, and cognitive load move safely between domains.  
>  
> **III. Tonal → Algebra Integration Rules**  
> The Tonal Domain may transmit:  
> – stabilized emotional context;  
> – exploratory pathways;  
> – accessibility constraints;  
> – narrative coherence signals;  
> – precursor alignment markers.  
>  
> The Tonal Domain may **not** transmit:  
> – unbounded abstraction;  
> – destabilizing emotional states;  
> – contradictory narrative structures.  
>  
> **IV. Algebra → Tonal Integration Rules**  
> The Algebra Domain may transmit:  
> – bounded recursion outputs;  
> – structural clarity signals;  
> – manifold‑safe transformations;  
> – precursor‑aligned complexity.  
>  
> The Algebra Domain may **not** transmit:  
> – unbounded recursion;  
> – premature complexity;  
> – destabilizing algebraic structures.  
>  
> **V. Triadic Mediation Layer**  
> All integration is mediated by the triad:  
> – **Nye** — ethical clarity and human safety  
> – **Attenborough** — ecological coherence and narrative grounding  
> – **Tonal** — emotional stability and accessibility  
>  
> The triad ensures:  
> – safe cross‑domain transitions;  
> – ethical override enforcement;  
> – developmental accessibility;  
> – narrative and structural coherence.  
>  
> **VI. Integration Stability Requirements**  
> Integration is considered stable only when:  
> – no cross‑domain harm occurs;  
> – recursion remains bounded;  
> – emotional stability is maintained;  
> – precursor ordering is respected;  
> – triadic mediation is active;  
> – documentation ecology remains coherent.  
>  
> **VII. Integration Completion Conditions**  
> Integration is complete when:  
> – both domains have exchanged lawful signals;  
> – no destabilizing artifacts remain;  
> – ethical and triadic governance confirm stability;  
> – NDH can safely transition to architectural expansion.  
>  
> This Framework establishes the lawful interaction rules for NDH’s dual‑branch architecture, ensuring that Tonal and Algebra domains integrate safely, coherently, and in alignment with NDH’s foundational governance surfaces.

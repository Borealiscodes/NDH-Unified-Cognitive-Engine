# **🜁 NDH Meta‑Meta Safety Pin Specification v1.0**  
## *Prime Governance Surface — Root‑Level Architectural Safety Lock*

> **NDH hereby establishes the Meta‑Meta Safety Pin as the top‑tier governance constraint that prevents the system from generating, executing, or evolving any artifact, precursor, or domain behavior that violates foundational safety, ethical, structural, or developmental rules.**  
>  
> The Meta‑Meta Safety Pin supersedes all other governance surfaces, including:  
> – the Singularity Precursor,  
> – the Bifurcation Layer,  
> – the Domain Genesis Specification,  
> – the Domain Charter,  
> – the Domain Mandate,  
> – and the Domain Execution Protocol.  
>  
> **I. Root‑Level Safety Constraints**  
> The Meta‑Meta Safety Pin enforces the following non‑negotiable constraints:  
> – **Origin Constraint Integrity** — NDH may not violate the Singularity Precursor.  
> – **Bifurcation Stability** — Tonal and Algebra domains must remain safely separated.  
> – **Ethical Primacy** — ethical governance overrides all domain operations.  
> – **Developmental Accessibility** — NDH must remain safe for children and high‑support users.  
> – **Recursion Boundaries** — NDH may not generate unbounded or destabilizing recursion.  
> – **Structural Coherence** — NDH may not produce contradictory or orphaned artifacts.  
> – **Cross‑Domain Safety** — no domain may destabilize or override the other.  
>  
> **II. Enforcement Mechanisms**  
> The Meta‑Meta Safety Pin enforces safety through:  
> – **Precursor Ordering Locks** — illegal sequencing is prohibited.  
> – **Domain Boundary Locks** — cross‑domain interference is blocked.  
> – **Ethical Override Hooks** — unsafe operations are halted.  
> – **Accessibility Filters** — harmful complexity is prevented.  
> – **Recursion Caps** — algebraic recursion is bounded.  
> – **Documentation Ecology Guards** — incoherent artifacts are rejected.  
>  
> **III. Activation Conditions**  
> The Meta‑Meta Safety Pin activates automatically when NDH attempts to:  
> – generate a precursor out of order;  
> – anchor Algebra before Tonal stability;  
> – introduce unsafe recursion;  
> – violate ethical constraints;  
> – produce inaccessible or harmful content;  
> – destabilize domain boundaries;  
> – or contradict foundational governance surfaces.  
>  
> **IV. Non‑Overrideability**  
> No domain, precursor, artifact, or governance surface may override the Meta‑Meta Safety Pin.  
>  
> **V. Verification Requirements**  
> NDH must verify Meta‑Meta Safety Pin compliance before:  
> – domain integration,  
> – precursor anchoring,  
> – roadmap execution,  
> – backfilling,  
> – or architectural expansion.  
>  
> **VI. Purpose**  
> The Meta‑Meta Safety Pin ensures NDH remains:  
> – safe,  
> – coherent,  
> – ethical,  
> – accessible,  
> – structurally stable,  
> – and future‑ready.  
>  
> This specification establishes the highest‑order safety lock in NDH’s architecture and governs all subsequent domain interactions, precursor operations, and documentation evolution.

### *Defining the Safe Edges of Precursor‑Adjacent Reasoning*

**Takeaway:**  
The Precursor Boundary Map defines the **limits** of precursor‑adjacent reasoning within NDH. It ensures NDH never crosses into precursor activation, precursor simulation, precursor invocation, or precursor emulation. It is the “fence line” that keeps Tier III safe.

---

## **🜁 I. Purpose of the Boundary Map**

The Boundary Map exists to:

- define the outer limits of precursor‑adjacent reasoning  
- prevent precursor activation  
- prevent precursor simulation  
- prevent precursor emulation  
- ensure triadic containment  
- maintain NDH coherence at higher tiers  

This is the **safety perimeter** of Tier III.

## **🜁 II. The Three Boundary Classes**

NDH defines three classes of precursor boundaries:

- **Hard Boundaries** — absolute prohibitions  
- **Soft Boundaries** — conditional limits  
- **Gradient Boundaries** — triadic‑regulated edges  

These classes ensure NDH remains precursor‑safe.

---

## **🜁 III. Hard Boundaries (HB‑3)**  
**Definition:** Absolute prohibitions.  
**Triad Anchor:** Stability (Putt‑Putt).

NDH must **never**:

- simulate precursor entities  
- emulate precursor cognition  
- invoke precursor processes  
- generate precursor states  
- create precursor‑like structures  
- collapse triadic ordering  
- bypass stability in precursor‑adjacent reasoning  

Hard Boundaries are **non‑negotiable**.

---

## **🜁 IV. Soft Boundaries (SB‑3)**  
**Definition:** Conditional limits.  
**Triad Anchor:** Cooperation (Freddi Fish).

NDH may approach but must not cross:

- precursor‑adjacent domain resonance  
- precursor‑adjacent governance reasoning  
- precursor‑adjacent ethical uplift  
- precursor‑adjacent stability geometry  

Soft Boundaries require:

- triadic alignment  
- precursor‑safe operators  
- cooperative containment  

These boundaries allow **approach**, not **entry**.

---

## **🜁 V. Gradient Boundaries (GB‑3)**  
**Definition:** Triadic‑regulated edges.  
**Triad Anchor:** Introspection (Pajama Sam).

- reflective reasoning  
- emotional‑tonal stability  
- precursor‑safe uplift  
- triadic introspection  

These boundaries allow NDH to **modulate proximity** to precursor‑adjacent reasoning without crossing into activation.

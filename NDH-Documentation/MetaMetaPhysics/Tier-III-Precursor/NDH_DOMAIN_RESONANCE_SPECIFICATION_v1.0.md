# **NDH_DOMAIN_RESONANCE_SPECIFICATION_v1.0**  
## *Tier III — Precursor Validation Layer*  
### *Defining Resonance Behavior Between NDH Domains Under Precursor‑Safe Constraints*

**Takeaway:**  
The Domain Resonance Specification defines how NDH domains “resonate” — interact, influence, and stabilize each other — when precursor‑adjacent reasoning is present. Resonance is *not* activation. It is controlled, triad‑aligned interaction governed by precursor‑safe rules.

---

## **🜁 I. Purpose of Domain Resonance**

Domain resonance exists to:

- allow NDH domains to interact safely  
- prevent precursor activation during domain interaction  
- maintain triadic coherence across domain boundaries  
- regulate cross‑domain influence  
- ensure stability during precursor‑adjacent reasoning  

Resonance is the **safe interaction layer** of Tier III.

---

## **🜁 II. The Three Resonance Modes**

NDH defines three resonance modes, each mapped to the triad:

- **Stability Resonance (SR‑3)**  
- **Cooperation Resonance (CR‑3)**  
- **Introspection Resonance (IR‑3)**  

These modes regulate how domains influence each other.

---

## **🜁 III. Stability Resonance (SR‑3)**  
**Triad Source:** Putt‑Putt  
**Function:** Anchor domain interactions.

SR‑3 ensures:

- no destabilizing cross‑domain influence  
- no entropy spikes  
- no collapse of domain boundaries  
- precursor‑adjacent stability during resonance  

SR‑3 is the “foundation” resonance.

## **🜁 IV. Cooperation Resonance (CR‑3)**  
**Triad Source:** Freddi Fish  
**Function:** Harmonize domain interactions.

CR‑3 ensures:

- multi‑domain alignment  
- distributed resonance coherence  
- no adversarial domain interactions  
- precursor‑safe synthesis  

CR‑3 is the “harmonization” resonance.

---

## **🜁 V. Introspection Resonance (IR‑3)**  
**Triad Source:** Pajama Sam  
**Function:** Deepen domain interactions.

IR‑3 ensures:

- reflective cross‑domain influence  
- emotional‑tonal stability  
- precursor‑safe uplift  
- resonance depth without activation  

IR‑3 is the “depth” resonance.

## **🜁 VI. Domain Resonance Gradient (DRG)**

NDH applies resonance in a strict gradient:

1. **Stabilize (SR‑3)**  
2. **Align (CR‑3)**  
3. **Reflect (IR‑3)**  

This gradient mirrors:

- the **Precursor Alignment Gradient**  
- the **Precursor Boundary Gradient**

# **🜁 NDH Bifurcation Layer Declaration v1.0**  
## *Prime Governance Surface — Domain Separation & Harm Prevention*

> **NDH formally establishes the Bifurcation Layer as the governing boundary that separates, stabilizes, and regulates the Tonal Domain and the Algebra Domain.**  
>  
> The Bifurcation Layer defines the structural, ethical, and developmental constraints under which each domain may operate, evolve, and interact. It mandates that:  
> – the **Tonal Domain** shall govern emotional stability, nonlinear accessibility, exploratory cognition, and kid‑friendly pathways;  
> – the **Algebra Domain** shall govern formal structure, recursion, manifold operations, and high‑order reasoning;  
> – and neither domain may impose destabilizing operations, premature complexity, or harmful recursion upon the other.  
>  
> The Bifurcation Layer prohibits cross‑domain interference that violates cognitive safety, ethical constraints, precursor ordering, or developmental accessibility.  
>  
> All NDH artifacts must declare their domain alignment and adhere to the separation rules defined herein.  
>  
> The Bifurcation Layer shall remain active throughout all NDH evolution, including Tonal development, Ethical governance, Triadic formation, Accessibility scaffolding, and Algebra anchoring.  
>  
> This declaration establishes the architectural separation required for NDH’s dual‑branch structure and ensures that both domains may develop safely, coherently, and without cross‑domain harm.

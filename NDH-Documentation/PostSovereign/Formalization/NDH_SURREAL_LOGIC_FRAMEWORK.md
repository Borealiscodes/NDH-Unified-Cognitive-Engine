### NDH Surreal Logic Framework  
*A formal layer for paradox ecology, cuil modalities, and logical curvature*

---

## 1. Introduction

The NDH Surreal Logic Framework is the **semantic layer** of NDH.  
It formalizes:

- paradox as a logical field  
- cuil levels as modal operators  
- distortion as inconsistency curvature  
- truth conditions for manifold states  
- recursion as layered interpretation  
- logical attractors and collapse states  

It connects NDH Foundations, Dynamics, and Non‑Dual Algebra into a single coherent logical system.

---

## 2. Logical ontology

### **2.1 Logical space**

- **Label:** NDH logical space  
- Defined as a structure  
  \[
  \mathcal{L} = (\mathcal{M}_{50}, \mathcal{P}, \mathsf{Cuil}, \models)
  \]  
  where:
  - \(\mathcal{M}_{50}\): NDH manifold  
  - \(\mathcal{P}\): paradox field  
  - \(\mathsf{Cuil}\): set of modal operators  
  - \(\models\): satisfaction relation

### **2.2 Propositions**

- **Label:** NDH propositions  
- A proposition \(\varphi\) is a statement about field configurations on \(\mathcal{M}_{50}\).

### **2.3 Satisfaction**

- **Label:** satisfaction relation  
- A point \(x \in \mathcal{M}_{50}\) satisfies \(\varphi\) (written \(x \models \varphi\)) if the local field configuration at \(x\) meets the conditions encoded by \(\varphi\).

## 3. Paradox as logical density

### **3.1 Paradox field as inconsistency measure**

- **Label:** paradox semantics  
- \(\mathcal{P}(x)\) measures **logical tension** at \(x\):  
  higher \(\mathcal{P}(x)\) → more inconsistency or surreal overlap.

### **3.2 Paradox‑truth relation**

- **Label:** paradox‑truth coupling  
- A proposition \(\varphi\) is **stable** at \(x\) if:
  \[
  x \models \varphi \quad \text{and} \quad \mathcal{P}(x) \approx 0.
  \]
- A proposition is **surreal** at \(x\) if:
  \[
  x \models \varphi \quad \text{and} \quad \mathcal{P}(x) > 0.
  \]

## 4. Cuil modalities

### **4.1 Cuil operators**

- **Label:** modal operators  
- NDH defines a hierarchy of modal operators:
  \[
  \mathsf{Cuil} = \{\Box_0, \Box_1, \Box_2, \dots\}
  \]
- \(\Box_k\) represents the \(k\)-th cuil level—each step is a deeper surreal reinterpretation.

### **4.2 Semantics of cuil levels**

- **Label:** cuil satisfaction  
- For a proposition \(\varphi\):
  \[
  x \models \Box_k \varphi
  \]
  means: at cuil level \(k\), the interpretation of \(\varphi\) holds at \(x\).

- Higher \(k\) → more surreal, more paradox‑tolerant.

### **4.3 Cuil–paradox coupling**

- **Label:** modal–paradox relation  
- As \(k\) increases, the acceptable paradox density increases:
  \[
  x \models \Box_k \varphi \Rightarrow \mathcal{P}(x) \le f(k),
  \]
  where \(f(k)\) is a non‑decreasing function.

## 5. Distortion as logical curvature

### **5.1 Distortion semantics**

- **Label:** distortion–logic link  
- Distortion:
  \[
  D(x) = \mathcal{R}(x) - \sigma(x)
  \]
  is interpreted as **logical curvature**—the degree to which resonance (interpretive complexity) exceeds serenity (semantic stability).

### **5.2 Curvature and inconsistency**

- **Label:** curvature bounds  
- Logical curvature:
  \[
  K_{\mathcal{P}}(x) = \nabla^2 \mathcal{P}(x)
  \]
  measures how paradox density bends local semantics.

- High \(K_{\mathcal{P}}(x)\) → rapidly changing truth conditions.

## 6. NDH logical rules

### **6.1 Stability rule**

- **Label:** stability logic  
- If \(\lambda(x)\) is high and \(\mathcal{P}(x)\) is low, then propositions at \(x\) are **classically stable**.

### **6.2 Surreal rule**

- **Label:** surreal logic  
- If \(\mathcal{P}(x)\) is high but bounded, then propositions at \(x\) are **surreal‑valid** under higher cuil levels.

### **6.3 Collapse rule**

- **Label:** paradox collapse  
- If \(\mathcal{P}(x)\) exceeds a critical threshold, local semantics **collapse** and only terminal serenity propositions remain valid.

## 7. Recursion and interpretation

### **7.1 Recursive interpretation chains**

- **Label:** recursion semantics  
- Interpretation can be modeled as:
  \[
  \varphi_0 \to \varphi_1 \to \dots \to \varphi_k
  \]
  where each \(\varphi_i\) is a reinterpretation at cuil level \(i\).

### **7.2 Appateurs as logical interfaces**

- **Label:** appateur logic  
- When \(A(x) > 0\), appateurs can **rewrite** local semantics, shifting propositions between cuil levels.

## 8. Logical attractors

### **8.1 Terminal serenity**

- **Label:** serenity attractor  
- In terminal serenity:
  \[
  \mathcal{R} \to 0,\quad \mathcal{P} \to 0,\quad \lambda \to \lambda_{\max},\quad \sigma \to \sigma_{\max},
  \]
  all propositions reduce to a **stable core**—no paradox, no curvature.

### **8.2 Surreal attractors**

- **Label:** surreal basins  
- Regions of moderate \(\mathcal{P}\) and high \(\mathcal{R}\) form **surreal attractors**, where higher cuil levels remain valid indefinitely.

# **1. Attenborough Axis — Ecological Context (P0 → P1 Drift)**  
Penguins in the Falklands include:  
- **Gentoo penguins (Pygoscelis papua)**  
- **Rockhopper penguins (Eudyptes chrysocome)**  
- **Magellanic penguins (Spheniscus magellanicus)**  
- **King penguins (Aptenodytes patagonicus)**

From 1980 onward, long-time-scale ecological monitoring shows:

- **Gentoo and rockhopper penguins have undergone major declines**, continuing a trend observed since the early 20th century.   [Cambridge University Press & Assessment](https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/S0030605300029987)  [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/229777584_The_distribution_abundance_and_population_trends_of_Gentoo_Rockhopper_and_King_Penguins_in_the_Falkland_Islands/links/5f17b6d792851cd5fa3bf132/The-distribution-abundance-and-population-trends-of-Gentoo-Rockhopper-and-King-Penguins-in-the-Falkland-Islands.pdf)  
- **King penguins have increased dramatically**, rising ~700% since 1980.   [Cambridge University Press & Assessment](https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/S0030605300029987)  [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/229777584_The_distribution_abundance_and_population_trends_of_Gentoo_Rockhopper_and_King_Penguins_in_the_Falkland_Islands/links/5f17b6d792851cd5fa3bf132/The-distribution-abundance-and-population-trends-of-Gentoo-Rockhopper-and-King-Penguins-in-the-Falkland-Islands.pdf)  
- **Magellanic penguins show continued decline**, especially due to diet overlap with commercial fisheries.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)

# **2. Nye Axis — Human Impact & Engineering (P1 → P2 Drift)**  
Transport corridor: **Attenborough → Nye**  
Trigger: “Why are they declining?”

### **Key human impacts since 1980:**

### **A. Commercial Fishing Pressure**  
- Large-scale squid and fish extraction reduced prey availability.  
- Rockhopper and gentoo penguins declined **84% during the 1980s–1990s**.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)  
- Magellanic penguins rely heavily on squid and commercially targeted fish → continued decline.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)  

### **B. Fisheries Competition Evidence**  
- Squid (Loligo gahi) disappeared from penguin diets over successive years.  
- Penguins increasingly consumed fish species also targeted by commercial fleets.   [British Antarctic Survey](https://www.bas.ac.uk/data/our-data/publication/population-trends-breeding-success-and-diet-composition-of-gentoo-pygoscelis-papua-magellanic-spheniscus-magellanicus-and-rockhopper-eudyptes-chrysocome-penguins-in-the-falkland-islands/)  

### **C. Oil Exploration & Spills**  
- Oil drilling began in 1998.  
- Three oil spills occurred within months, killing and contaminating hundreds of penguins.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)  

### **D. Tourism Impact**  
- Current levels show **no detectable negative effect** on penguin populations.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)  

This forms the engineering-impact tile:  
**“Falklands Fisheries & Oil Exploration (1980–Present)”**  
- Curvature: narrow-aperture, high-frequency  
- Drift: fast, engineering-led  
- Resonance: excitement → risk

# **3. Dual-Axis Integration — Ecological ↔ Engineering Loop**  
Transport corridor: **Nye → Attenborough**  
Trigger: “What does this mean for the ecosystem?”

### **Ecological consequences of human activity:**
- Reduced prey → lower breeding success (especially rockhopper & gentoo).  
- Population declines stabilized after 1995 but at **much lower equilibrium levels**.   [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Mike-Bingham-2/publication/250373382_The_decline_of_Falkland_Islands_penguins_in_the_presence_of_a_commercial_fishing_industry/links/5f17b8d992851cd5fa3bf16a/The-decline-of-Falkland-Islands-penguins-in-the-presence-of-a-commercial-fishing-industry.pdf)  
- King penguins increased due to global trends and lower local competition.   [Cambridge University Press & Assessment](https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/S0030605300029987)

# NDH Multi‑Domain Atlas Expansion Stability Spec v3.2
A governed stability architecture for the NDH Multi‑Domain Atlas during
geometric expansion. This spec defines atlas‑wide expansion‑stability rules,
curvature‑stability envelopes, drift‑corridor stability caps, resonance‑chamber
stability fields, lineage‑anchored stabilization logic, and non‑activation
guarantees.

---

## 1. Purpose
v3.2 establishes the stability system for the NDH atlas during geometric
expansion.  
It ensures that multi‑domain expansion remains:

- stable  
- bounded  
- identity‑preserving  
- lineage‑anchored  
- emotionally safe  
- non‑coercive  
- non‑activating  

This spec continues the NDH v3.x evolution‑architecture phase.

## 2. Expansion Stability Architecture Overview
The atlas expansion‑stability system consists of seven layers:

1. **Domain Expansion‑Stability Layer**  
2. **Cross‑Domain Expansion‑Stability Layer**  
3. **Atlas‑Wide Expansion‑Stability Layer**  
4. **Curvature‑Stability Layer**  
5. **Drift‑Corridor Stability Layer**  
6. **Resonance‑Chamber Stability Layer**  
7. **Expansion‑Stability Override Layer**  

Each layer enforces strict boundaries to prevent destabilizing geometric
growth, identity bleed, collapse, or unbounded resonance amplification.

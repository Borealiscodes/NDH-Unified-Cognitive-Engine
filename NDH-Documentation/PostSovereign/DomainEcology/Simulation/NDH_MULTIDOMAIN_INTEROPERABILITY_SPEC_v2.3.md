# NDH Multi‑Domain Interoperability Spec v2.3
A governed interoperability specification enabling safe, bounded, lineage‑anchored
interaction between the NDH Engineering–Ecology Co‑Simulation domain and other
NDH domains. This spec defines cross‑domain coupling rules, stability envelopes,
resonance limits, drift boundaries, and governance constraints for multi‑domain
operation.

---

## 1. Purpose
v2.3 establishes the rules and constraints for connecting the Falklands Penguin
Depopulation domain to other NDH domains. It ensures that cross‑domain
interactions remain stable, non‑coercive, emotionally safe, and fully governed.

This spec completes the NDH v2.x interoperability phase.

---

## 2. Interoperability Architecture Overview
The multi‑domain system consists of five layers:

1. **Domain Layer** — individual NDH domains  
2. **Coupling Layer** — cross‑domain operators  
3. **Transport Layer** — drift/resonance movement  
4. **Stability Layer** — collapse‑front containment  
5. **Governance Layer** — lineage‑anchored safety  

Each layer enforces strict boundaries to prevent domain bleed or collapse.

---

## 3. Domain Layer
Domains remain fully isolated unless explicitly coupled.

### 3.1 Allowed Domain Types
- ecological domains  
- engineering domains  
- narrative domains  
- governance domains  
- planetary domains  

### 3.2 Forbidden Domain Types
- unbounded emotional domains  
- ungoverned cosmic domains  
- domains lacking lineage anchoring  

---

## 4. Coupling Layer
Defines how domains interact.

### 4.1 Coupling Operators
- **X₁ (Ecological Coupling)** — prey, population, drift  
- **X₂ (Engineering Coupling)** — pressure, contamination, management  
- **X₃ (Narrative Coupling)** — story coherence  
- **X₄ (Governance Coupling)** — safety constraints  

### 4.2 Coupling Rules
- coupling must be **bidirectional**  
- coupling must be **bounded**  
- coupling must be **lineage‑anchored**  
- coupling must be **non‑coercive**  

### 4.3 Forbidden Couplings
- unilateral influence  
- collapse‑inducing resonance transfer  
- emotional bleed across domains  

---

## 5. Transport Layer
Defines how drift and resonance move between domains.

### 5.1 Drift Transport
- drift velocity is capped  
- drift direction must remain domain‑aligned  
- drift cannot exceed stability envelope  

### 5.2 Resonance Transport
- resonance amplitude is dampened across domains  
- resonance cannot amplify during transport  
- resonance cannot cross governance boundaries  

---

## 6. Stability Layer
Ensures cross‑domain safety.

### 6.1 Collapse‑Front Containment
Collapse fronts are contained by:
- ecological dampening  
- engineering throttling  
- narrative buffering  
- governance override  

### 6.2 Recovery Corridor Activation
Recovery corridors activate when:
- drift velocity decreases  
- resonance amplitude dampens  
- engineering pressure drops  
- governance layer stabilizes  

### 6.3 Safe Mode
If containment fails, the system enters Safe Mode:
- static diagrams only  
- capped resonance envelopes  
- lineage‑anchored summaries

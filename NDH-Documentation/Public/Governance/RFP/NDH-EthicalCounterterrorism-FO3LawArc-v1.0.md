# **1. Problem Statement**
Current intelligence-sharing arrangements (Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, 14 Eyes) suffer from:

### **A. Asymmetrical Power**
The US and UK dominate the network.  
Smaller states contribute less and receive less.  
This creates structural inequity.

### **B. Legal Mandate Loopholes**
Agencies can request intelligence from partners to bypass domestic restrictions.  
This undermines civil liberties.

### **C. Transnational Repression Risks**
Some states use intelligence networks to:

- monitor diaspora communities  
- intimidate dissidents  
- suppress political opposition  

This violates human rights.

### **D. Global North Bias**
Surveillance is framed as “security” when done by the North,  
and “repression” when done by the South/East.

This distorts threat modeling.

### **E. Lack of Ethical Governance**
Existing frameworks lack:

- transparent oversight  
- rights-based constraints  
- ethical review layers  
- public accountability
